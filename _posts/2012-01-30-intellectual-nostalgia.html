Tonight I was absolutely delighted to have found an archive of James Sedgwick's The Certainty Site. It is an excellent, accessible guide to the Objectivist epistemology and I fondly remember checking it frequently as it was being created. I'm so glad someone preserved it.

Now if only someone had done the same for E.G. Ross' The Objective American. That was an exceptional daily essay on wide-ranging topics written in the 90s that did not outlive its creator.
